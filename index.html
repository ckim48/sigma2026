About

Project 3Sigma

Project 3Sigma is a multidisciplinary initiative developed in collaboration with the Seneca Park Zoo Primate Portal to better understand animal minds and cognitive behavior.

By integrating custom hardware like pallet-based food dispensers with cutting-edge machine learning facial recognition, we enhance the efficiency and scalability of cognitive studies. Our mission is to enable reliable data collection that serves both the scientific community and public education.

Clients

Academic Partner

The Primate Portal

A collaborative research initiative hosted by Carnegie Mellon University. The lab focuses on the evolutionary origins of human cognition, using touchscreen-based tasks to study learning, memory, and logic in primates.

Cognitive Research

Developing benchmarks for cross-species intelligence comparisons.

Data Visualization

Translating complex primate behavior into actionable research datasets.

Institutional Partner

Seneca Park Zoo

Located in Rochester, NY, Seneca Park Zoo provides the physical infrastructure and animal care necessary for non-invasive research. It serves as a bridge between the scientific community and public wildlife conservation education.

Animal Welfare

Ensuring all cognitive experiments are voluntary and enrichment-focused.

Public Engagement

Bringing live science to zoo visitors and local school districts.

Selective Engineering Team

Project 3Sigma employs a very rigorous selection process to identify elite middle and high school students who demonstrate exceptional talent in their respective fields.

Our student engineers collaborate across multidisciplinary domains—integrating Science, Robotics, Engineering, and Digital Arts—to develop practical technological solutions, such as custom hardware and machine-learning systems for cognitive research.

Deliverables

Engineering Cognitive Research Systems

The Robotics & Engineering team designed and implemented a fully autonomous experimental platform that enables non-invasive cognitive testing in primates.

Students engineered custom robotic food dispensers, integrated sensor-based interaction logic, and developed computer vision pipelines for facial recognition and behavioral tracking.

One such component is a robotic pallet-style dispenser that delivers cookie-based rewards in response to correct task execution, supporting reliable behavioral reinforcement during touchscreen-based experiments.

  • Custom robotic hardware & enclosures
  • Pallet-based cookie reward dispenser
  • ML-based primate face recognition
  • Scalable data collection infrastructure
